Word Note
ingenuous a complimentary term
frank, open, artless, not likely to try to put anything over on you, nor apt to hide feelings or thoughts that more sophisticated persons would consider it wise, tackful, or expedient to conceal
disingenuous not frank or open, crafty, cunning, dishonest, artful, insincere, untrustworthy. And they are all of these while making a pretense of being simple, frank, and aboveboard.
ingenious shrewd, clever, inventive
ingenuousness
ingenuity
naïve a derogatory term
to imply that they have not learned the ways of the world, and are thereforeidealistic and trusting beyond the point of safety; such idealism and trust have probably come from ignorance or inexperience
naïveté
credulous a derogatory term
a willingness to believe almost anything, no matter how fantastic
usually results from ignorance or inexperience, or perhaps from an inability to believe that human beings are capable of lying
Latin credo: to believe
credulity
gullible a derogatory term
easily tricked, easily fooled, easily imposed on
stronger than credulous, and is more derogatory
results more from stupidity than from ignorance or inexperience
gullibility

Latin acuo: to sharpen
acute sharp, sudden
acuity
acupuncture the insertion of a needle into the body for medical purposes
punctus: point
punctuate When you punctuate a sentence, you put various points (pediods, commas, etc.)where needed
When lightning punctuates the storm, or when the silence is punctuated by the wailing of police sirens, points interrupt the atmosphere, the quiet
punctual right on the point of time
punctuality the noun form of punctual
punctilious exact, scrupulous, very careful to observe the proper points of behavior, procedure
puncture to make a hole in it with a sharp point
figuratively, to break illusions, fantasies, or ego, tire
pungent another form of the root punctus (pungo, to pierce sharply)
having a sharp, spicy smell or taste or wit
pungence the noun form of pungent
pungency the noun form of pungent

introspection

care; watchfulness, caution
